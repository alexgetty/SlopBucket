# First-Person Experiment

"I tried X so you don't have to" journalism. The author subjects themselves to something and reports findings. Results are always transformative. Sample size is always one.

## Headline Templates

- "I [Did Thing] For [Time Period]. Here's What Happened."
- "I Tried [Thing] So You Don't Have To"
- "I Spent [Time] [Doing Thing]. It Changed Everything."
- "What Happened When I [Action] For [Duration]"
- "I [Extreme Action] For A Week. Here's What I Learned."
- "I Quit [Thing] For [Time]. The Results Were [Adjective]."
- "I Let [Person/Thing] [Control Something] For [Time]"
- "I Lived Like [Type Of Person] For [Duration]"

## Example Headlines

- "I Said 'Per My Last Email' For A Month. Here's What Happened."
- "I Tried Being Offline So You Don't Have To"
- "I Quit Having Opinions For A Week. It Changed Everything."
- "What Happened When I Agreed With Everyone For 30 Days"
- "I Let The Algorithm Run My Life. The Results Were Concerning."

## Article Structure

- Explain why you did this (thin justification)
- Day-by-day or phase-by-phase breakdown
- Moment of crisis/doubt in the middle
- Breakthrough realization (unearned)
- What I learned (obvious or absurd)
