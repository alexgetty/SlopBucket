# Opinion/Editorial

First-person hot take with unearned confidence. The byline implies authority. The content delivers delusion. Maximum gravitas, minimum self-awareness.

## Headline Templates

- "Opinion: [Controversial Take Stated As Fact]"
- "I [Did Thing]. Here's Why You Should Too."
- "[Thing] Is [Hot Take]. There, I Said It."
- "It's Time We Talk About [Thing Everyone Already Talks About]"
- "Why [Popular Thing] Is Actually [Negative Adjective]"
- "Opinion: We Need To Stop [Thing No One Is Doing]"
- "[Thing] Changed My Life. It Could Change Yours."
- "I'm Just Going To Say What Everyone Is Thinking: [Personal Opinion]"

## Example Headlines

- "Opinion: Your Coffee Order Is A Personality Disorder"
- "I Quit Reply-All And It Saved My Marriage"
- "Meetings Are Productivity. There, I Said It."
- "It's Time We Talk About Hustle Culture (Again)"
- "Why Being Bad At Things Is Actually A Superpower"

## Article Structure

- Open with "I" statement establishing unearned authority
- Anecdote that proves nothing but feels meaningful
- Pivot to broader societal implications
- Dismiss counterarguments without addressing them
- End with call to action that's impossible to follow
- Byline: [Name], [Impressive But Irrelevant Credential]
