# Explainer

The "here's what you need to know" format. Implies the reader is confused about something everyone already understands. Condescending helpfulness meets unnecessary complexity.

## Headline Templates

- "[Topic], Explained"
- "What Is [Obvious Thing]? Everything You Need To Know"
- "[Topic]: A Beginner's Guide"
- "Everything You Need To Know About [Simple Thing]"
- "[Topic] 101: What It Is And Why It Matters"
- "What Is [Thing] And Why Is Everyone Talking About It?"
- "The Complete Guide To [Thing That Needs No Guide]"
- "[Thing], Explained Like You're 5 (Because Apparently You Need That)"

## Example Headlines

- "Meetings, Explained"
- "What Is Email? Everything You Need To Know"
- "Coffee: A Beginner's Guide For Millennials"
- "What Is 'Going Outside' And Why Is Everyone Talking About It?"
- "The Complete Guide To Having An Opinion"

## Article Structure

- Lead: Establish that this is confusing (it isn't)
- "What is [thing]?" section (defines the obvious)
- "Why does it matter?" section (inflates importance)
- "Key terms to know" sidebar (unnecessary jargon)
- "What experts say" (vague quotes)
- "The bottom line" (restates the headline)
