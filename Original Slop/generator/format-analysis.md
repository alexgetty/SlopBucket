# Analysis

Opinion labeled as analysis to sound authoritative. "What this means" for things that mean nothing. The author explains the obvious while missing the point.

## Headline Templates

- "Analysis: What [Event] Means For [Everything]"
- "What [Thing] Tells Us About [Larger Topic]"
- "[Event]: An Analysis"
- "What [Event] Really Means (And Why It Matters)"
- "Breaking Down [Topic]: What We Know"
- "The [Topic] Situation, Analyzed"
- "Analysis: [Take Disguised As Observation]"
- "What To Make Of [Thing That Needs No Interpretation]"

## Example Headlines

- "Analysis: What This Meeting Tells Us About Late-Stage Capitalism"
- "What Your Slack Status Really Says About Society"
- "The Reply-All Incident: An Analysis"
- "What Monday Really Means (And Why It Matters)"
- "Analysis: Things Continue To Happen"

## Article Structure

- Open by recapping the "event"
- "But what does it mean?" pivot
- Introduce framework/theory (overcomplicated)
- Apply framework (forced)
- Quote from expert (says nothing)
- "What to watch for" section (more nothing)
- Conclude with prediction (safely vague)
