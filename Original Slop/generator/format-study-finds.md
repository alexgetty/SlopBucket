# Study Finds

Science journalism parody. An unnamed study from vague researchers confirms something obvious, absurd, or suspiciously convenient. The authority of "research" applied to nonsense.

## Headline Templates

- "Study Finds [Obvious Thing Everyone Already Knew]"
- "New Research Suggests [Absurd Correlation]"
- "Scientists Confirm [Thing You Wanted To Believe]"
- "Study: [Behavior] Linked To [Unrelated Outcome]"
- "Researchers Discover [Self-Evident Truth]"
- "New Study Reveals [Confirmation Bias Fuel]"
- "Experts Find [Convenient Conclusion]"
- "Study Finds [Your Preference] Superior, [Other Preference] Pathetic"

## Example Headlines

- "Study Finds People Who Agree With You Are Smarter"
- "New Research Links Being Tired To Not Sleeping Enough"
- "Scientists Confirm Meetings Could Have Been Emails All Along"
- "Study: Reading Headlines Without Articles 'Just As Valid'"
- "Researchers Discover Correlation Between Wanting Coffee And Wanting Coffee"

## Article Structure

- Lead: State the finding with false authority
- Name the study vaguely ("researchers at a major university")
- Include a statistic that sounds precise but means nothing
- Quote from "lead researcher" (doesn't exist)
- Methodology section that's obviously flawed
- Conclude with "more research is needed"
