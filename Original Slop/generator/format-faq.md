# FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions that no one has asked. Pre-emptive defense disguised as helpfulness. The questions are softballs. The answers are evasions.

## Headline Templates

- "[Topic] FAQ: Your Questions Answered"
- "Frequently Asked Questions About [Thing]"
- "[Number] Questions You Definitely Have About [Topic]"
- "[Topic]: Answers To Questions You Didn't Ask"
- "Everything You Wanted To Know About [Topic] (But Were Afraid To Ask)"
- "[Topic] FAQ: What You Need To Know"
- "Your [Topic] Questions, Answered"
- "[Topic]: The Questions Everyone Is Asking (They're Not)"

## Example Headlines

- "Hustle Culture FAQ: Your Questions Answered"
- "Frequently Asked Questions About Our Definitely Real Company Values"
- "7 Questions You Definitely Have About Meetings"
- "Reply-All: Answers To Questions You Didn't Ask"
- "Everything You Wanted To Know About 'Synergy'"

## Article Structure

- Brief intro claiming demand for clarity
- Q&A pairs where Q's are softballs
- A's that answer different questions than asked
- One "tough" question with deflecting answer
- Circular references to other FAQs
- "Didn't find your answer?" with contact info (won't help)
