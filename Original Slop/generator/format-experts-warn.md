# Experts Warn

Fear-mongering journalism. Unnamed experts issue dire warnings about trivial things. Maximum alarm, minimum specificity. The experts are never named because they don't exist.

## Headline Templates

- "Experts Warn [Trivial Thing] Could [Catastrophic Outcome]"
- "Scientists Concerned About [Non-Issue]"
- "Health Officials: [Normal Behavior] May Be [Dangerous]"
- "Experts: [Thing You Do] Linked To [Thing You Fear]"
- "Warning: [Activity] Could [Vague Threat]"
- "Experts Say [Convenient Thing To Worry About]"
- "[Professionals] Alarmed By [Trend That Isn't Happening]"
- "New Concerns Emerge Over [Eternal Constant]"

## Example Headlines

- "Experts Warn Your Morning Routine May Be Destroying Shareholder Value"
- "Scientists Concerned About Rise In 'Having Opinions'"
- "Health Officials: Skipping Meetings May Cause Unclear Consequences"
- "Experts: Reading This Article Linked To Wasted Time"
- "Warning: Productivity Could Be Hazardous To Productivity"

## Article Structure

- Alarming lede with no specific details
- Quote from "leading expert" (unnamed)
- Statistic that sounds scary but means nothing
- "Critics argue..." (brief dismissal of sanity)
- Return to fear with "but experts maintain..."
- End with ominous "only time will tell"
